This Week's Theme

The interface is becoming the intelligence — as AI shifts from prompted interactions to constraint-driven systems, design teams must radically redefine both "user" and "usability."

🗞️  Week in Context

Three convergent signals this week: prompting as interaction paradigm is dying, AI agents are becoming users themselves, and human-made design is emerging as the new trust differentiator. The field is grappling with a fundamental ontological shift — when intelligence becomes distributed across agents and interfaces, traditional user-centered design assumptions break down. Teams are caught between accelerating AI capabilities and slowing comprehension cycles, creating what one observer calls "obscure UX" — interfaces that ship faster than they can be understood. The response isn't more sophisticated prompting but more sophisticated constraints.
